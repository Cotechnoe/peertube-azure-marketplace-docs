# Privacy Policy — Cotechnoe PeerTube on Azure Marketplace

**Publisher:** Cotechnoe  
**Effective date:** 2026-06-15  
**Last updated:** 2026-06-15

---

## 1. Overview

This privacy policy applies to the **Cotechnoe PeerTube — Video Platform and Federated Streaming Server** offer published on Microsoft Azure Marketplace.

This offer consists of a pre-configured Azure virtual machine image. Cotechnoe does not operate the PeerTube instance on your behalf. Once deployed, the virtual machine runs entirely within **your own Azure subscription**, under **your control**.

---

## 2. Data collected by Cotechnoe

Cotechnoe does **not** collect, store, or process any personal data through the virtual machine image itself.

The VM image does not contain:
- Telemetry or analytics agents reporting back to Cotechnoe
- Remote access backdoors or monitoring tools
- Any pre-configured credentials tied to Cotechnoe infrastructure

---

## 3. Data collected by Microsoft Azure

Deploying a VM from Azure Marketplace is subject to Microsoft's own privacy practices:

- [Microsoft Privacy Statement](https://privacy.microsoft.com/en-us/privacystatement)
- [Azure Marketplace Terms of Use](https://azure.microsoft.com/en-us/support/legal/marketplace-terms/)

Microsoft may collect deployment telemetry (subscription ID, region, offer ID) as part of the standard Marketplace transaction process.

---

## 4. Data processed by your PeerTube instance

Once deployed, your PeerTube instance is **your responsibility**. PeerTube is open-source software developed by Framasoft. The data processed by your instance (user accounts, uploaded videos, comments, federation activity) is stored in your VM's disk and your Azure subscription.

Relevant upstream privacy references:
- [PeerTube documentation](https://docs.joinpeertube.org)
- [Framasoft legal notices](https://framasoft.org/en/legals/)

As the operator of a PeerTube instance, you are the **data controller** under applicable law (including GDPR if you serve users in the EU). You are responsible for publishing your own privacy policy to your instance users.
